Hayes is owned in just 0.5% of leagues. That is the number. He was a 60%+ owned player last season. The market has written him off entirely. The luck model says that's exactly when to be buying.

**Luck score: +0.570. BABIP: .129. wOBA: .209. xwOBA: .308. Ownership: 0.5%.**

## This Week's Sell Highs

### Riley Greene, OF, DET — *86.4% owned*

Greene's BABIP (.424) is the story here. It's running more than 130 points above his career norm. When BABIP sits that high without a corresponding xwOBA spike to justify it, that's luck — and luck doesn't hold.

His xwOBA (.399) is actually solid — he's not a bad player. But wOBA (.386) is slightly outrunning even that. The model flags this as a Sell High, which means his trade value is currently at a peak the underlying data doesn't support.

At 86.4% owned, he's widely held. If someone in your league wants to give you full value for him based on his BABIP-inflated counting stats, the signal says take the deal.

**Luck score: -0.350. BABIP: .424. wOBA: .386. xwOBA: .399. Ownership: 86.4%.**

## Pitcher Signal of the Week

### Clay Holmes, RP, NYM — *58.5% owned*

Holmes' ERA (1.69) looks spectacular. His underlying numbers say something different. FIP: 3.55. xERA: 3.65. LOB%: .894 — strand rate in the 90th percentile, which is extraordinarily unsustainable.

The BABIP allowed (.210) is also below his career rate (.279), meaning hitters are getting less fortunate than usual. Both pieces of luck — the strand rate and the opponent BABIP — are compressing his ERA well below what his peripherals support.

Holmes is a good reliever. He is not a 1.69 ERA reliever. If someone wants to buy high on his closer role and current ERA, the signal says sell.

**Luck score: -0.506. ERA: 1.69. FIP: 3.55. xERA: 3.65. LOB%: .894. Ownership: 58.5%.**

## The Trade Analyzer — Three Scenarios

This week we used the live Signal Trade Analyzer on three real scenarios circulating in leagues right now. Each one tells a different story about how signals and scarcity interact.

---

### Scenario 1 — The "Skenes Is Untouchable" Trap

**You give:** José Ramírez + Joe Ryan  
**You receive:** Paul Skenes

**Verdict: AVOID**

Ramírez is a Buy Low with an elite scarcity premium — FP #6 overall. Ryan is also a Buy Low. Skenes has a neutral signal: his ERA legitimately reflects his peripherals. The model isn't down on Skenes. It's very bullish on what you'd be giving away.

Trading away two strong buy signals — one of them a top-6 overall player whose market value is suppressed right now — to acquire a clean neutral signal is not a good trade. The names make it feel reasonable. The math says decline it.

---

### Scenario 2 — The 2-for-1 Temptation

**You give:** Corey Seager  
**You receive:** Matt Chapman + Dillon Dingler

**Verdict: SLIGHTLY UNFAVORABLE**

Here's the nuanced one. Seager is a Buy Low. Chapman is a Sell High. Dingler is a Buy Low. You're selling a buy signal and absorbing a sell signal in the same transaction — that's two signal headwinds in one move.

Dingler at catcher is a genuine value add. But he alone doesn't offset giving up a Buy Low SS in Seager. There's also a hidden cost: receiving two players means dropping one from your roster. The tool calculates that cost explicitly.

The model's verdict is SLIGHTLY UNFAVORABLE. If you like the depth angle, restructure: ask for Dingler plus a pitcher, not Chapman.

*This is the scenario type worth showing your leaguemates. It's not an obvious AVOID — it requires understanding why you'd give up a buy signal to get a sell signal. That's exactly what the tool is built for.*

---

### Scenario 3 — The Textbook Signal Trade

**You give:** Brice Turang  
**You receive:** Joe Ryan + José Ramírez

**Verdict: STRONG TRADE**

Three green lights. Turang is a Sell High — his stats are running ahead of his contact quality, and he's nearly 98% owned, which means his market value is at its peak. Ryan and Ramírez are both Buy Lows. Ramírez also carries an elite scarcity premium: he's FP #6 overall, and his market value is suppressed right now because his results haven't caught up to his underlying quality.

The delta on this trade is substantial. There's a small roster cost (you drop one waiver player), but the signal alignment is as clean as it gets. Give peak value on a Sell High. Load up on two Buy Lows, one of whom is an elite player trading at a discount.
